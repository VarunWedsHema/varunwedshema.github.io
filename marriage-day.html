Thalappoli :

A welcome with flowers and lights. Women or young girls carry thalam, which literally means plate, in their hands. Thalam is filled with rice, flowers and a lighted lamp

Punyaham

This is done just before the actual marriage function.The Punyaham ritual includes mantras to the effect that gods will be pleased and will support blessings by pious seniors / elders who are themselves pleased through such rituals.This will also purify the surrounding groom and bride.

Nandimukham – "Aashir Namasthiya Vasthu Nirddeshova Thanmukham"

The marriage ceremony will start from here .Bride changes her dress to the one which the groom's party brings ("manthra kodi"). The bride gets a Valkannadi ( Mirror made of Brass ) . The bride is supposed to hold this in her hand till the wedding ceremony concludes. The bride is brought to the Mandap and seated facing east direction.

Thaalikettu (tying the wedding pendant)

Bride's father puts these pendants in a thread and does "punyaham" chanting mantra. The men / boys celebrate this merrily by shouting "aarrrppoooo, hirroo..hirroo" repeatedly. At the same time, women do "kurava", a sound produced with the twisting of the tongue, denoting happiness.

Aayiram thiri and Panakkudam Uzhiyal

The thousand threads prepared by the bride are lit and is moved in front of the bride in a circular manner. This is done to get rid of any effects of evil eyes. The same is held for the groom with a pot of water (panakkudam uzhiyal).

After this ceremony, the father of the bride takes along the bride to the venue of "homam". The girl is accompanied by "ashtamangalyam" – eight signs of prosperity in a marriage. Ashtamangalyam consisting of the following items: water filled pot (nirakudam), cloth (vasthram), decorated mirror (valkannadi), kajal (kanmashi), sindoor (sindooram / kumkumam), Sandal paste (chandanam), and ten sacred flowers (dasha pushpam).Bride then hands over the tulsi garland she had prepared before wedding to the groom, who adores the same around his neck.

Malar varukkal (preparation of puffed rice):

During the wedding ceremony, there is a function called "laja homam". For this, the preparation of puffed rice is done by the bride herself.

Paanigrahanam

The bridegroom holds the hand of the bride while chanting manthras. The paanigrahanam ritual symbolizes the bride surrendering her heart in the hands of the groom during the occasion of the marriage.

Saptha Padi

During this ritual, the groom circles around the sacred fire along with his bride from right side. All along, he holds his wife's right hand in his right hand in the way in which he held her hand during the paani grahanam ceremony. He stops, bends down and holds the right toe of his wife with his right hand and helps her take seven steps around the fire.

Sadya (Grand Feast )– By Pazhayidom Mohanan Namboothiri

Sadya is a variety of pure vegetarian dishes traditionally served on a banana leaf in Kerala. In our marriage this is coordinating by the famous chief PAZHYIDOM MOHANAN NAMBOOTHIRY. He is a world famous cook for vegetarian meals. He conducted feasts in all part of the india and abrode. He is the main chief for State Yuvajanolsavam where lakhs of people attending .He is pleased to conduct sadya in the marriage function with his magicalvegetarian feast.
